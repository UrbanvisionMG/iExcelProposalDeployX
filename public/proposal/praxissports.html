1 Executive Summary

Praxis Sports is a sports tech platform that simplifies scheduling, communication, and operations for clubs, academies, and teams - from youth to pro. The product is strong and the team is uniquely credible (Olympians, MLS veterans), but the digital footprint is early-stage and fragmented:

  • Organic search is thin beyond branded terms; a handful of pages drive most traffic.
  • Social channels (especially Instagram/TikTok) show promise, but content is not yet tied to a clear funnel or narrative.
  • There is no paid acquisition currently running.
  • Tracking, attribution, and GEO/SEO foundations can be strengthened to support scale.

At the same time, Praxis has clear strategic goals:

  • Tell one coherent story about who you are, who you serve, and why you're different.
  • Grow a community of clubs and leaders around that story.
  • Turn that community into a predictable pipeline of demos, trials, and accounts - while respecting startup budgets.

  • ✓ Build the digital foundation: on-page SEO + GEO, conversion-focused web structure, and rock-solid tracking.
  • ✓ Stand up first scalable acquisition engines: bottom-of-funnel paid search, lightweight paid social, and targeted outbound (cold email + LinkedIn).
  • ✓ Optimize for revenue and adoption, not vanity metrics - weekly cycles of "what we did, what we learned, what we're doing next."

2 What We Learned

Business & product

  • Praxis is a sports management platform: scheduling, communication, operations; aimed at simplifying the chaos of youth and team sports.
  • You serve a broad spectrum (youth clubs, academies, schools, pro environments), but the messaging and web experience don't yet make that breadth feel coherent.
  • Praxis is priced below many competitors, but that advantage is not clearly or consistently communicated.
  • The team is a moat: co-founders with MLS experience, CRO with Olympic gold medal credentials, deep relationships in the sports ecosystem - yet this story is underused in marketing.

Current marketing reality

Website & SEO

  • Small index footprint; most organic traffic is branded or near-branded.
  • Rankings have been volatile in recent months; non-branded discovery is limited.
  • Site structure and content are not yet mapped to how club directors, DOCs, or operations staff search (e.g., "youth soccer scheduling software," "sports management app for clubs").

Social media

  • Active presence on Instagram and TikTok with product clips, team stories, and educational posts; LinkedIn/X are earlier-stage but important for B2B buyers.
  • Content is promising, but there's no clear bridge from social attention → owned traffic → demo / trial.

Paid media

  • No live paid search or paid social campaigns; all traffic is organic or direct/referral.

Measurement & infrastructure

  • Basic analytics exist, but tracking is not yet treated as a growth product (consistent event tracking, funnel views, and reporting for marketing decisions).

Goals & constraints

build a community and brand around Praxis while driving measurable growth in qualified demos/accounts.

Timing

align with 2026 planning; you're in a growth stage and starting to invest externally.

Constraints

4 Tactical Plan

Phase 1 - Narrative, Foundations & Tracking (Weeks 1 - 4)

1. Narrative & audience alignment

  • 90-minute Narrative & Audience Workshop with Lindsay + key team.
  • Deliver Praxis Narrative Pack:
  • 1-page positioning summary (problem → solution → who Praxis serves).
  • Audience snapshots (youth club director, DOC, college coach, academy operations).
  • Key proof points (team credibility, pricing advantage, product strengths).
  • Guardrails: what to emphasize/avoid in marketing language.

2. Website / funnel baseline

  • Audit current site structure, UX, and forms.
  • Map core journeys: first-time visitor → learn → social proof → pricing → demo/trial.
  • Identify 2 - 3 priority landing experiences (e.g., "Club Ops", "Coaches", "Multi-Sport Orgs") for redesign or refinement.

3. Tracking & data layer

  • Implement / clean up GA4 + Google Tag Manager:
  • Events for demo form views/submits, trial signups, pricing clicks, "book a call", etc.
  • UTM standards for all channels.
  • Configure a simple marketing performance dashboard (Looker Studio or similar).

Phase 2 - SEO + GEO & Site Optimization (Weeks 5 - 10)

1. SEO Site Optimization (existing pages)

  • Update titles, meta descriptions, H1s/H2s, and internal linking for:
  • Home, Product/Features, Pricing, "Who We Serve", key blog posts/resources.
  • Add FAQ sections and structured data where appropriate.
  • Fix technical hygiene: sitemap, robots, canonical tags, core web vitals where feasible.

2. GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)

  • Implement JSON-LD schema, llm.txt file, and concise summary blocks that LLMs can easily ingest.
  • Add 8 - 12 high-quality FAQ Q&As focused on "what is Praxis," "who uses it," "how it's priced," etc., to improve answers in AI search.

3. Content roadmap

  • Build a 6-month editorial roadmap of high-intent topics (e.g., "How to run a 400-team tournament without losing your mind", "Sports ops checklist for club admins").
  • Ship 2 - 4 initial blog/guide pieces to start compounding organic traffic.

Phase 3 - Paid Search & Paid Social Activation (Weeks 8 - 16)

(Phase 3 overlaps Phase 2 intentionally.)

1. Paid Search - Google Ads

  • Research and select bottom-of-funnel keyword clusters around:
  • "sports management software", "youth club scheduling software", "team communication app", etc.
  • Build campaigns with:
  • 2 - 3 tightly themed ad groups per cluster.
  • Responsive Search Ads (12 - 15 headlines, 4 descriptions) using Praxis' value props.
  • Landing pages matched to intent and persona.
  • Implement conversion tracking to qualified demo/trial, not just raw form fills.

2. Paid Social - Meta & LinkedIn (lean tests)

  • Create 3 - 5 creative concepts using existing Praxis assets (product screens, testimonials, clips from Lindsay/MLS co-founder).
  • Launch:
  • Meta campaigns for top/mid funnel (awareness and traffic).
  • LinkedIn campaigns targeting club executives, DOCs, operations leads.
  • Add retargeting (site visitors, engaged social users) to move prospects to demo.

Phase 4 - Outbound & Community Engine (Months 4 - 6)

1. Cold email engine

  • Build or refine a prospect list of clubs/academies/leagues by region and sport.
  • Write 2 - 3 cold email sequences (3 - 5 touches each) emphasizing problems solved, social proof, and a soft CTA ("15-minute walkthrough").
  • Set up sending via your existing tools or a lightweight outbound platform; ensure compliance and deliverability.

2. LinkedIn community building

  • Optimize Lindsay's and other leaders' LinkedIn profiles around Praxis narrative.
  • Create a connection + DM playbook targeting club and league decision-makers.
  • Repurpose top-performing TikTok/Instagram content into LinkedIn posts with more B2B framing.

3. Continuous optimization

  • Weekly growth cycles: review metrics, identify top-performing channels/creatives, kill losers, and reallocate budget.
  • Quarterly planning to align with sports calendar (tryouts, tournament season, back-to-school, etc.).

Brand & community

  • Praxis positioned as a credible, modern sports ops platform in both search results and AI-powered answers.
